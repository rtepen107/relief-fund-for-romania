Audience and Goals

After conducting the preliminary research, the target audience and the goals best suited to meet the needs of such groups defined.

Audience

Pinpointing the audience and goals required starting broad and narrowing down to the specifics.

The first step was to list the target groups in need and then list those the site would draw in in order to help. The audience members would most likely be donors of some kind or an orginization looking to partner with us. The Donors would be the most desirable audience followed by those spreading the word by way of social media.

Orphans, orphanages and donors were paired together as the most need because those were the two grouos that need the most aid and would give the most aid, respectively. The next focus group would be the disabled and social media because these areas did not have much focus. Later, I realised that impoverished families would be more of a secondary focus than the disabled minority.





Goals

Once the audience and target groups were defined, each group, including the website, were given major goals. These overarching goals were then broken down into more specific groups with desired actions and required content.

Overarching Goals

Client
  • Bring in Donations
  • Educate and raise awareness
  • Find and Support Donors
Audience
  • Donate
  • Be educated
  • Share information and message through social media
  • volunteer
Website
  • Streamline and simplify donation and sponsorship options
  • Give clear and necessary information and history
  • Organize needs by prority and what makes sense to the audience
  • Simplify and organize projects and partnerships

Specific Goals

User Goal Content/Action
Partner Orginizations Seek partnerships for similar projects, provide services, direct donations Contact to become a partner/partners page
Churches Volunteer, spread information, sponsor projects Volunteer page/button, Sponsor child/family, Donate to projects, volunteer at a pop-up, host a clothing drive
Adults Donate, Sponsor, Be educated/spread info Information for projects, History page, Sponsor or one time donations, follow on social media.
Young Adults Be informed/spread info, follow social media, volunteer, buy from pop-ups. Social media links, easy access information, pop-ups page (volunteer/host, donate clothes)

Goals Conclusion

The Relief Fund for Romania seeks to direct and provide aid to those in need in Romania. This exercise helped narrow down the groups that the organization should focus on, and in what priority. It became apparent that there was a large need for a social media presence, in order to both educate and also give updates for events or projects. These goals were done before any interviews, so the priority of certain groups and actions would shift slightly, but most stayed the same.

The new site would need clear and concise information placed appropriatly first. There would need to be updated or new pages such as sponsorships, a more organized donation page and a Pop-Ups page. The key would generally have to be how information was delivered both in form and tone. The placement of these elements in order to attract the attention of the right audience would also need to be sorted.
